Cowrywise Mobile App Redesign Case Study
(Focus on Discover Circles flow)


Company's Overview

Cowrywise is a financial technology startup based in Lagos, Nigeria. Cowrywise automates your savings and investment, taking away the stress and planning required to do such on a regular basis. Your plans generate interests daily over the period you specify

 Objective

 To redesign the “Discover circles flow” of Cowrywise app in a way that enhances the
 overall experience of visitors and converts them to everyday users.

Affinty Mapping

I affinitized the gathered data into themes and developed insights and design principles.

Research Trends

  1. Description should be clear.
  2. There are biases in school selection.
  3. Withdrawal date should reflect amount.
  4. There should be emergency withdrawal for the groups.
  5. Illustrations should be replaced with expressive images and clear visuals.
  6. Users should be allowed to create groups too.
  7. Segments should be made components.

 Testing Insights

I conducted testing on the two  variations of the wireframes and here are
some of the insights.

  1. Loves design accessibility.
  2. Limited feedback because it’s low-fi.
  3. Extra field for secondary students and Married couple.
  4. Wants emergency withdrawal for the groups.
  5. Preferred first design more.
  6. Users should be allowed to create groups too.

 High Fidelity Testing

I moved the first variation to high fidelity and took some screens that aligns well with users from the second variation. I conducted another round of interview with 4 participants where I place it side by side with the current experience. The majority went with
the redesigned.

 Iterations

Below are the highlights of the major changes implemented in the redesign

  1. Added create challenge feature
  2. Replaced Batch C with NYSC, that way, it captures all that are serving and not just a batch.
  3.  Went with the first variation based on user feedback. I replaced some screens that were not clear to the users.
  4. Showed activities so that first visitors can see people on the saving challenge
  5. Narrowed it to undergraduate circle to capture all universities and polytechnics.
  6. Recreated content to "Singles can save" so as to capture all that does not identify with any gender.

 Final Design

Although the redesigned was preferred, there was some iterations carried out to ease user flow of the new design.
